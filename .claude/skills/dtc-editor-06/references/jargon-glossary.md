## Marketing Acronyms

| Jargon | Plain English |
|--------|---------------|
| ROAS | return on ad spend (how much profit you make per dollar spent on ads) |
| AOV | average order value (what customers typically spend per purchase) |
| LTV | customer lifetime value (total revenue you'll earn from one customer over time) |
| CLV | customer lifetime value (same as LTV) |
| CAC | customer acquisition cost (how much you spend to get one new customer) |
| CPA | cost per acquisition (what you pay for each sale or signup) |
| CTR | click-through rate (percentage of people who click your ad or link) |
| CVR | conversion rate (percentage of visitors who actually buy) |
| CRO | conversion optimization (improving your site so more people buy) |
| CPM | cost per thousand views (what you pay for 1,000 people to see your ad) |
| CPC | cost per click (what you pay each time someone clicks your ad) |
| CLTV | customer lifetime value (same as LTV) |
| MER | marketing efficiency ratio (total revenue divided by total ad spend) |
| POAS | profit on ad spend (like ROAS but uses profit instead of revenue) |
| RPV | revenue per visitor (average revenue each website visitor generates) |

## Marketing Terms

| Jargon | Plain English |
|--------|---------------|
| funnel | the steps customers take from first seeing you to buying |
| sales funnel | the journey from stranger to customer |
| conversion funnel | path visitors take to becoming buyers |
| top of funnel | people just discovering your brand |
| middle of funnel | people considering your product |
| bottom of funnel | people ready to buy |
| TOFU | awareness stage (people just learning about you) |
| MOFU | consideration stage (people comparing options) |
| BOFU | decision stage (people ready to purchase) |
| retargeting | showing ads to people who already visited your site |
| remarketing | same as retargeting |
| lookalike audience | new people similar to your existing customers |
| custom audience | a group of people you define for targeting |
| attribution | figuring out which ad or channel caused a sale |
| multi-touch attribution | crediting multiple ads that led to one sale |
| pixel | code on your website that tracks visitor behavior |
| tracking pixel | same as pixel |
| conversion tracking | measuring when visitors take desired actions |
| impression | one person seeing your ad one time |
| reach | total number of unique people who saw your content |
| frequency | how many times each person sees your ad |
| engagement | likes, comments, shares, clicks on your content |
| organic | free traffic/reach (not paid advertising) |
| paid | advertising you pay for |
| earned media | free coverage from press, influencers, word of mouth |

## E-Commerce Terms

| Jargon | Plain English |
|--------|---------------|
| DTC | direct-to-consumer (selling directly to customers, no middleman) |
| D2C | same as DTC |
| SKU | product code (unique identifier for each product variation) |
| fulfillment | getting orders packed and shipped to customers |
| 3PL | third-party logistics (a company that stores and ships your products) |
| dropshipping | selling products you don't physically stock |
| inventory | the products you have in stock |
| cart abandonment | when shoppers add items but don't complete purchase |
| checkout abandonment | same as cart abandonment |
| upsell | offering a better/more expensive version |
| cross-sell | suggesting related products |
| bundle | multiple products sold together at a discount |
| subscription | recurring automatic purchases |
| churn | customers who stop buying or cancel subscriptions |
| retention | keeping existing customers buying |
| repeat purchase rate | percentage of customers who buy again |
| AOV lift | increasing the average amount customers spend |
| margin | profit after costs (what you keep after expenses) |
| gross margin | revenue minus product costs |
| net margin | profit after ALL expenses |
| break-even | the point where you cover all costs |
| cash flow | money coming in minus money going out |

## Technical Terms

| Jargon | Plain English |
|--------|---------------|
| A/B test | comparing two versions to see which works better |
| split test | same as A/B test |
| multivariate test | testing multiple changes at once |
| API | connection between two software systems |
| integration | connecting two tools to work together |
| automation | tasks that run automatically without manual work |
| workflow | sequence of automated steps |
| landing page | the page visitors arrive on after clicking an ad |
| product page | the page showing a single product |
| collection page | page showing multiple products in a category |
| homepage | your main website page |
| above the fold | content visible without scrolling |
| below the fold | content you need to scroll to see |
| CTA | call-to-action (button or link telling visitors what to do) |
| hero section | the large prominent area at the top of a page |
| social proof | reviews, testimonials, customer counts that build trust |
| UGC | user-generated content (photos and videos from real customers) |
| mobile-responsive | website that works well on phones |
| page speed | how fast your website loads |
| bounce rate | percentage of visitors who leave immediately |
| session duration | how long visitors stay on your site |
| heatmap | visual showing where people click and scroll |

## Business/Strategy Terms

| Jargon | Plain English |
|--------|---------------|
| scale | grow your business significantly |
| leverage | use something to get more results |
| optimize | improve to get better results |
| iterate | make small improvements over time |
| pivot | change your strategy or direction |
| vertical | your specific industry or niche |
| niche | specific segment of the market you target |
| TAM | total addressable market (everyone who could buy) |
| ICP | ideal customer profile (your perfect customer) |
| persona | fictional representation of your target customer |
| positioning | how customers see you compared to competitors |
| value proposition | why customers should choose you |
| USP | unique selling proposition (what makes you different) |
| moat | competitive advantage that's hard to copy |
| runway | how long your money will last |
| burn rate | how fast you're spending money |
| unit economics | profit/loss on each individual sale |
| KPI | key performance indicator (important number to track) |
| OKR | objectives and key results (goal-setting framework) |
| ROI | return on investment (profit compared to what you spent) |

## Email Marketing Terms

| Jargon | Plain English |
|--------|---------------|
| open rate | percentage of people who open your email |
| click rate | percentage of people who click a link in your email |
| deliverability | whether your emails reach the inbox |
| bounce | email that couldn't be delivered |
| hard bounce | invalid email address |
| soft bounce | temporary delivery failure |
| unsubscribe | when someone opts out of your emails |
| list hygiene | removing inactive or invalid email addresses |
| segmentation | dividing your email list into smaller groups |
| personalization | customizing emails for each recipient |
| automation | emails that send automatically based on triggers |
| drip campaign | series of emails sent over time |
| welcome sequence | emails new subscribers receive automatically |
| abandoned cart email | reminder about items left in shopping cart |
| win-back campaign | emails to re-engage inactive customers |
| ESP | email service provider (tool you use to send emails) |

## Advertising Platform Terms

| Jargon | Plain English |
|--------|---------------|
| ad set | group of ads with the same targeting |
| campaign | collection of related ad sets |
| creative | the actual ad image, video, or text |
| copy | the written text in your ad |
| headline | main text that grabs attention |
| hook | opening that captures interest |
| bid | how much you're willing to pay for an action |
| budget | total amount you'll spend on ads |
| daily budget | maximum spend per day |
| lifetime budget | total spend for entire campaign |
| optimization | letting the platform find best results |
| broad targeting | reaching a wide audience |
| narrow targeting | reaching a very specific audience |
| interest targeting | targeting based on what people like |
| behavioral targeting | targeting based on what people do |
| demographic targeting | targeting based on age, gender, location |
| exclusion | people you don't want to see your ads |
| placement | where your ad appears |
| format | type of ad (image, video, carousel, etc.) |

## Quick Reference: Most Common Replacements

For fast editing, here are the terms you'll replace most often:

| Instead of... | Write... |
|---------------|----------|
| "optimize your funnel" | "improve your checkout process" |
| "leverage UGC" | "use customer photos and videos" |
| "increase AOV" | "get customers to spend more per order" |
| "improve ROAS" | "make more money from your ads" |
| "reduce CAC" | "spend less to get each new customer" |
| "boost CTR" | "get more people to click" |
| "higher CVR" | "turn more visitors into buyers" |
| "scale your ads" | "spend more on ads that work" |
| "retarget cart abandoners" | "show ads to people who left items in their cart" |
| "build a lookalike" | "find new people similar to your customers" |
