Your biggest competitor just launched a new product. You're scrambling to figure out their traffic sources, ad spend, and whether you should pivot.

Smart operators use a different approach.

They set up automated competitor monitoring that does the detective work while they sleep. One founder I know discovered his competitor's $50,000/day winning ad creative within 2 hours of it going live. He created a variation, launched it the same day, and pulled $12,000 profit in the first week.

The trick is combining three free tools most people use separately.

Start with Facebook Ad Library for creative monitoring. But instead of checking manually, use a Chrome extension called AdSpy Alerts (free version works fine). Set it to ping you whenever your top 3 competitors launch new campaigns.

Next, connect Google Alerts for their brand mentions and product launches. Set up alerts for "[competitor name] + discount," "[competitor name] + review," and their exact product names. You'll catch their PR pushes, influencer partnerships, and seasonal promotions before your customers do.

Finally, use Wayback Machine's "Save Page Now" feature on their landing pages weekly. This archives their exact copy, pricing, and offers. When they change something, you'll see what worked before they optimized it away.

The real power happens when these three systems work together. You get a notification about a new ad, cross-reference it with recent PR mentions, then check their archived landing pages to see if they changed their offer structure.

Set this up once on a Sunday afternoon. Takes maybe 20 minutes total.

Within two weeks, you'll have a competitive intelligence system that most million-dollar brands don't even run. You'll spot their winning moves before they scale them. You'll see their failed experiments before they pivot away. You'll know their seasonal strategy before their customers do.
